## Omelet rice / ketchup
###### Serves 2
#### Ingredients
##### Omelet Rice
- 100g chicken thighs
- 2-4 mushrooms
- 4 eggs
- 3tbsp ketchup
- 1/2 onion (100g)
- 260g cooked rice
- 20g butter
- Pinch of salt
##### Ketchup
- 1 whole tomato (400g)
- 2tbsp Sugar
- 1/2 onion (400g)
- 1tsp salt
- 1/2tbsp vinegar
###### Group A
- 3g garlic (grated)
- 3g ginger (grated)
- 1 bay leaf
- 1/4 - 1/5 stick cinnamon
- 1 clove
- 3 pepper corns
- 1/4 chili pepper (seeds removed)

#### Steps
##### Omelet Rice
1. Cut the chicken into 1cm strips. Dice the onion adn slice the mushrooms
2. Melt 10g butter in a frying pan and frythe onion. Then add the chicken and as it turns white add the mushrooms. When the mushrooms start to soften sprinkle the salt and add the ketchup
> Ketchup brings out the fragrance and keep it moist!
3. Add rice to **Step 2** and fry, mixing everything together well. Then serve on plates
> Use high heat! Reommened 20cm frying pan
4. Beat 2 eggs well (for 1 person). Melt 5g butter in pan and add beaten egg.
5. Stir egg from the edges towards teh center. When teh egg has set turn off the heat. The finished egg will still be soft on top
6. Move the pan to **Step 3** and slide the omelet on top of the rice mixture using a rubber spatula
##### Ketchup
1. Roughly chop the onion, add to blender with tomato and paste
2. Heat **Step 1** in a pan. Bring to boil then reduce temperature, stirring occasionally for 30 minutes. Add a mix and boil well
3. Sieve **Step 2** and remove the spices
4. Return **Step 3** to the pan. Add salt and sugar, simmer for 5 minutes. Mixture will thicken. When it turns glossy, add vinegar. Simmer on very low heat 1-2 minutes. Done!

## Chicken and cabbage
###### Serves 2-3
#### Ingredients
- 800g cabbage (1/4 of 1 large head)
- 300g chicken thighs
- 50cc sake
###### Group A
- 6g grated garlic
- 2tbsp (30cc) soy sauce

#### Steps
1. Cut the cabbage into 3-4cm chunks. Separate the leaves from teh core. Cut the thicker parts in half. Remove moisture
2. Slice the chicken into 12 equal pieces
3. Lay the chicken skin side down in a pan and pour sake over the top. Add the cabbage core pieces, cover with a lid and cook
4. Cook over a high heat for 5 minutes. Stir once then add the remaining leaves
> During **Step 4** the chicken should turn white
5. Cook for another five minutes and you're done! Cover in **Group A** and enjoy!
> Only using a little sauce exemplifies the umami. When simmering make sure it doesn't burn and lose all its moisture over the high heat!

## Pizza / miso onion burdock pizza / vegetable garden pizza
###### Makes 4 24cm pizzas (2 of each pizza)
#### Ingredients
##### Pizza (base)
- 190cc Warm water (30°C)
- 3g dry yeast
- pinch of sugar
- 1tbsp olive oil
###### Group A
- 150g flour
- 150g strong flour
##### Miso onion burdock pizza
- 30cm (70) burdock
- 20cm (60g) green onion
- 100g mozzarella cheese
- 6 beefsteak leaves
- 1/2tbsp sesame oil
###### Group A
- 30g miso
- 70cc mirin
##### Vegetable Garden Pizza
###### Group A
- 1/2 zucchini
- 1/4 bell pepper
- 2 pinches salt
- 1/2 eggplant
- 1 cove of garlic
- 100g mozzarella cheese
##### Tomato Sauce
- 1/2 can diced tomatoes (200g)
- 1/2tbsp olive oil
- 1/tsp salt

#### Steps
##### Pizza (base)
1. Dissolve the sugar in 30cc of warm water, add yeast, leave for 15 minutes (to ferment)
2. Combine **Group A** in a large bowl. Dissolve the salt in the remaining water
3. Hollow out the center of the flour. Add **Step 1**, **Step 2**, and the olive oil. Gradually incorporate the flour in the bowl
4. Move the dough to a clean counter and knead
5. Knead for about 15 minutes, fold edges into center until the dough is a ball. Pinch it closed
6. Lightly grease a large bowl with olive oil. Place dough in bowl pinched side down. Leave for 1 to 1.5 hours in a warm place to rise
7. When the dough has risen to about twice the size, place on floured surface. Use hands to push out excess gas, then divide into 4 pieces
8. Fold the edges of each piece into the center and pinch closed. Place on a tray and cover in a damp cloth. Leave for 15 minutes
9. Once topppings are prepared place dough on floured surface. Push down with hands. Using rolling pin to roll out dough into 24cm base. Pizza base is done!
##### Miso onion burdock pizza
1. Slice the burdock into thin slices, add sesame oil and fry for 3-4 minutes. Slice onion into thin slices.
2. Combine **Group A** in small pan and simmer until everything is dissolved
3. Pre-heat an empty baking tray in oven at 250°C. Place a pizza base on a piece of baking paper. Sprinkle **Step 2** on top, then arrange burdock and onion from **Step 1** with the mozzarella cheese
4. Take the tray out of the oven and place the baking sheet with pizza on top. Bake at 250°C for 7 minutes. Remove from oven, add the leaves and serve!
##### Vegetable garden pizza
1. Thinly slice al ingredients of **Group A** into 2-3mm strips. Combine ingredients for the tomato sauce. Pre-heat an empty baking tray in oven at 250°C
2. Place pizza base on baking sheet. Spread 2-3tbsp tomato sauce on top
3. Cover with finely chopped garlic, zucchini, eggplant, and pepper. Sprinkle vegetables with salt and mozzarella
4. Take the tray out of the oven and place the baking sheet with pizza on top. Bake at 250°C for 8-9 minutes. Then serve
> Try baking this pizza without the sauce!

## Sesame short bread / boule de neige
###### Makes 30
#### Ingredients
##### Sesame Short Bread
- 75g flour
- 50g butter
###### Group A
- 25g raw sugar
- 10g white sesame
##### Boule De Neige
- 60g flour
- 50g unsalted butter
- 25g almond powder
- 15g sugar

#### Steps
##### Sesame Short Bread
1. Let the butter come to room temperature
2. Sift the flour into a bowl and add the butter from **Step 1**. Cut and combine the flour and butter until it becomes similar to bread crumbs. Once combined add Group A
3. Fold and mix the dough until it becomes uniform. As it begins to take shape mold it into forms
> It's okay if it falls apart a little at this point!
4. Cover the top with cellophane and spread the dough, pushing down hard with a table spoon
5. Remove the cellophane, bake in pre-heated oven at 150°C for 40 minutes
6. Once baked, score with a knife and leave to cool. Enjoy once it's completely cooled
##### Boule De Neige
1. Bring butter to room temperature
2. Add **Step 1** to bowl and cream with whisk. Add the sugar and whisk
3. Add the flour and almond powder. Use a rubber spatula to fold mixture together. Don't knead it!
4. Once the powder is all gone, carefully combine the crumbs together
5. Put **Step 4** on to cellophane on a chopping board, then shape. Leave in fridge for 1-2 hours
6. Split **Step 5** into 30 pieces and shape into balls
7. Bake **Step 6** in 160°C for 20 minutes
8. Once baked, leave to cool (cooling rack). Sprinkle with sugar (5-10g) while warm. Leave to cool
9. When cool put in plastic bag and add more sugar (5g). Shake the bag to cover with sugar

## Spring vegetable rice / sea bream soup / steamed turnip sea bream
###### Serves 4
#### Ingredients
##### Spring Vegetable Rice
- 150g turnip leaves
- 50g Japanes eparsley leaves
- 1-1.5tsp salt
- 2 cups uncooked rice
##### Sea Bream Soup
- 400g sea bream (no head or tail)
- Dash of sake
- 1L water
- Some 5x5cm kombu
- Low sodium soy sauce to taste
##### Steamed Turnip Sea Bream
- 2 sea bream fillets (200g)
- 600g turnip
- 1 egg white
- 1/2tsp salt
- Pinch wasabi
###### Paste
- 200cc bonito stock
- 2tsp low sodium soy sauce
- 2tsp mirin
- 2tsp potato starch (dissolved in 2tsp water)

#### Steps
##### Spring Vegetable Rice
1. Boil plenty of water in pan. Season with salt (not from ingredients). Remove the well washed turnip leaves from the stock, boil for 2 minutes. Rinse with plenty of cold water. Squeeze out water. Chop into 5mm.
2. Boil parsley for 10 seconds, rinse and cut the same way
3. Combine **Step 1** and **Step 2**. Just before the rice is finished mix in the salt. Leave for 5 minutes for remaining moisture to evaporate
4. Mix **Step 3** into the rice, and it's done! Adding sesame or dried whitebait is good too!
##### Sea Bream Soup
1. Put water into pan and add kombu
2. Rinse the sea bream and pat dry with paper towel. Lightly salt and leave to sit. Then broil on both sides
3. Add **Step 2** and vinegar to **Step 1**, remove kombu before it boils. Remove scum after boiled, lower heat and simmer for 10 minutes
4. Remove the scum again. Add the soy sauce for taste!
> Add seasonal fragrances! Parsley and ginseng in spring, bamboo shoots and wokame, etc...
##### Steamed Turnip Sea Bream
1. Grate turnip, put the sieve. Use your hands to push the moisture out
2. Remove bones from sea bream, cut into 4. Sprinkle with salt and let sit.
3. Add egg white to **Step 1** and mix well
4. Divide **Step 2** into bowls for each person. Put **Step 3** on top. Steam for 10 minutes
5. Put **Group A** into pan and simmer. Add potato starch and make into paste
6. Top **Step 4** with **Step 5** once steamed. Add wasabi and done!
